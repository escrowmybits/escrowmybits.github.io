Welcome to Escrow my Bits, (“EMB”) the online escrow service provider for regular and multi-signature cryptocurrency transactions.
Escrow my Bits uses cryptocurrency transactions and multi-signature services to allow users to enter into an agreement (such as online commerce) with any other user, and to make sure that if the transaction fails, a selected third party ('judge') will be able to withhold payment. This intermediary trust service will add certainty to your online financial transactions. 
EMB Gives users the confidence to proceed with Online Purchases and virtual currency transactions by maintaining escrow of funds on your behalf.
EMB provides the capabilities to support Online Escrow Services, while we do not guarantee the quality of any online purchases or judgements, we do ensure quick, secure, and accurate completion of financial transactions once any disputes are resolved. Escrow my Bits does not guarantee the quality or fairness of the judges on our site, however the site will provide users with tools to publicly rate and review judges.

Once the terms of the transaction are finalized, and the transaction is executed, the cryptocurrency shall be released by the consent of 2 out of 3 from the selling party, the receiving party and the Judge or in the case of a transaction that does not utilize the Multi-Signature feature the cryptocurrency shall be released by Escrow my Bits or the selected Judge. 
If any dispute arises, then the Judge's ruling shall be final unless an appeal is filed prior to the end of a 3(three) day period starting from the time the Judge makes a ruling. Should an appeal be filed, the filing party will be required to make a small deposit that is equal to a small percentage of the transaction. The percentage will be decided on a case by case basis and once set cannot be changed. Appeal rulings are final.  Depending on the ruling, either a refund to the filing party will be made for the deposit, or the deposit from the filing party will be kept.
